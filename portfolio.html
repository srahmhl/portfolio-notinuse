DESIGN PORTFOLIO

A case study of my portfolio on my portfolio? Seems ironic, but I designed my portfolio research-first to appeal to my users: hiring managers and recruiters.

OBJECTIVES

  • Research industry standards and portfolio examples.
  • Design and wireframe the website.
  • Prepare the wireframes for “developer handoff.”
  • Build the website.

FINAL PROTOTYPE

You’re on it! But there are some slight differences between the prototype and the live website based on my coding abilities.

The high-fidelity prototype.

DESIGN METHODS

Hiring managers look at dozens if not hundreds of portfolios, resumes, and cover letters during a single hiring round, so their pain points are the basis of my design decisions. How can I make my portfolio fun and exciting while still showing my skills?

INITIAL WIREFRAMES

Ever play the game Stardew Valley? It was a huge source of inspiration, but based on user tests, I needed to make my own characters and my own atmosphere. The retro computer style also clashed.

SECOND PROTOTYPE

I thought I was done, but after more user tests, this time with real recruiters, I discovered that my portfolio was not optimized for their enjoyment or for their time. Recruiters and hiring managers spend about 3 minutes or less on a portfolio (and about 6 seconds on a resume), so it was back to the drawing board.

CONCLUSIONS

I iterated my portfolio so many times I got dizzy, but I learned to push through. A design is never truly finished, and as my users’ needs change, so will my portfolio.
