POCKET TEACHER

Pocket Teacher, a local start up, contacted me about building a prototype for their company. They’re looking for investors, so a prototype was the first step to building their business.

scroll for more

OBJECTIVES

  • Conduct market research.
  • Build initial wireframes and conduct user testing.
  • Create a style guide and build the final prototype.

FINAL PROTOTYPE

DESIGN METHODS

Pocket Teacher’s team had market research, ideas, and some initial wireframing, but not much else. It was up to me to create a prototype that would fulfill their needs. I started with sitemapping and design sprints.

Before I began, I knew I'd need a map of where the app was going to go and potential paths a user could take. This is why I made a site map before I started wireframing.

INITIAL WIREFRAMES

I met with the project manager online to ensure I was meeting the company’s goals and to answer any questions I was having during the design process.

CONCLUSIONS

The US spends about $56 billion a year on education technology (EduTech), so I wasn’t just designing for the app’s users--I was designing for possible stakeholders and investors. As Pocket Teacher grows, an excellent user experience will ensure their journey to success.
